‚õµ Triangular Trade

Triangular Trade connected Europe, Africa, and the Americas in a cycle of trade:

  • New England: Sent rum & goods to Africa
  • Africa: Sent enslaved Africans to the Americas
  • West Indies: Shipped sugar & molasses back to New England

üìú Navigation Acts & Colonial Tensions

The Navigation Acts were laws passed by England to control colonial trade. Colonists were required to trade only with England, use English ships, and pay taxes on imports.

These rules limited profits for colonists and led to widespread smuggling. Over time, frustration grew.
